⚠ Scoliosis Protocol
Due to cervical/upper thoracic scoliosis: no heavy overhead pressing, no behind-the-neck movements, no asymmetrical spinal loading. Every exercise is chosen with this in mind. If any movement causes neck or shoulder pain — stop immediately and do an extra row or lat pulldown instead.

Progression Roadmap
Weeks 1–4
Foundation. Learn every movement pattern. Don't chase weight. Completing every session with good form is the only thing that matters.
Weeks 5–8
Build. Small weight increases where comfortable. Push reps to the top of each range. Mobility sessions should feel noticeably easier.
Weeks 9–12
Consolidate. Train at 7–8/10 effort. Keep a workout notebook. Re-assess at week 12 — expect visible strength and body composition changes.

Zone 2 — What It Means
Zone 2 = you can hold a full conversation but it feels slightly uncomfortable. This is peak fat-burning territory and builds aerobic base without wrecking recovery. Combined with 10,000 steps daily, this creates a massive calorie deficit.
Cycle Session
Tuesday & Saturday
Cycling — 25 to 30 min
Warm-up — 5 min
Easy pace, minimal resistance. Let your body wake up gradually.
Steady State — 15–20 min
Zone 2 effort. Conversational but working. Moderate resistance. Stay consistent — don't sprint.
Cool Down — 5 min
Very easy pace, zero resistance. Slow your breathing before you stop completely.
Core Circuit — 3 Rounds
Dead Bug — 8 reps each side
Lower back pressed to floor throughout. No half reps.
Forearm Plank — 30 seconds
Breathe steadily — don't hold your breath. Hips level.
Glute Bridge — 15 reps
Squeeze and hold 1 second at the top of each rep.
Side Plank — 20 sec each side
Rest 60 sec, then repeat the full circuit. 3 total rounds.
10,000 Steps — Your Biggest Advantage
~450 kcal
At 98kg, 10,000 steps burns roughly 400–500 calories per day — around 3,000 per week. This compounds massively over months alongside your training.
Low Stress
Walking doesn't raise cortisol or strain recovery. You can walk every single day — it actually speeds up muscle recovery rather than hindering it.
UP Weather
Walk in early morning (6–8am) or evening (7–9pm). Avoid midday walks in summer. On very hot days, replace outdoor steps with extra time on the cycle.

130g protein as a vegetarian is achievable, but requires strategy. Soya chunks (Nutrela) are your biggest weapon — 52g protein per 100g dry weight, widely available in Prayagraj, and extremely cheap. Eat them daily. Supplement with paneer, dal, curd, and sattu to hit your target.

Meal 1 · 7–8am · Breakfast
Sattu Sharbat + Besan Chilla / Poha
2 tbsp sattu in water with jeera and kala namak + 2–3 besan chilla with paneer stuffing. Or: poha with peanuts and green peas. Light, high protein, perfect for UP mornings.
~28–32g protein
Meal 2 · 12–1pm · Lunch (Biggest Meal)
Dal + Soya Sabzi + Roti + Salad
2 roti (atta) + 1 bowl arhar or masoor dal + soya chunk sabzi (60–70g dry soya soaked overnight, cooked with masala) + large kheera-pyaaz salad. This one meal delivers the most protein of your day.
~45–50g protein
Snack · 4–5pm · Pre or Post Workout
Curd + Fruit + Peanuts or Makhana
1 bowl thick dahi + seasonal fruit (aam, kela, amrood, jamun) + 30g peanuts or roasted makhana. Easy to prep, no cooking. Great for recovery after training.
~18–22g protein
Meal 3 · 7–8pm · Dinner

1 bowl rajma or chole + 2 roti + seasonal sabzi (lauki, baingan, tori, methi — whatever is available) + 1 glass milk before bed. Rajma-chawal is fine but swap rice for roti to control calories.

6 Rules — Non-Negotiable
1
Soya chunks every day. This single food will do more for your protein intake than anything else. Soak overnight, then cook in any sabzi. Available at every kirana shop in Prayagraj.
2
Cut sugar, not meals. No chai with 3 spoons sugar, no cold drinks, no daily biscuits. Replace with sattu sharbat or nimbu paani with rock salt. Never skip meals — you will overeat at night.
3
Reduce ghee — don't remove it. 1 tsp per meal maximum. Ghee is not bad — the quantity is the issue. Avoid vanaspati/dalda entirely — it is harmful trans fat found in many UP street foods.
4
Vegetables at every meal. Sabzi is your best friend. Load up on lauki, tori, baingan, palak, methi, gobhi — all calorie-light, filling, and packed with micronutrients. Season freely with normal masalas.
5
Roti over rice when cutting. 2–3 atta roti per meal is perfectly fine. Rice is not bad but is much easier to overeat — keep portions small. White or brown rice, doesn't matter much.
6
Track your food for at least 2 weeks. Use any free app (even a basic one). Most Indians significantly underestimate how much ghee and oil goes into cooking. Seeing the numbers changes behaviour.
Foods to Prioritise vs Avoid
Eat More
Sattu, soya chunks, paneer, moong sprouts, rajma, chole, dahi, seasonal sabzi, atta roti, dal of any kind, peanuts, makhana
Limit
Ghee (1 tsp/meal max), rice portions, full-fat mithai, white bread, maida items like paratha made with refined flour
Avoid
Daily samosa/kachori, cold drinks, sugary chai, packaged namkeen, bakery biscuits, vanaspati-fried street food. These silently add 500+ calories per day.
